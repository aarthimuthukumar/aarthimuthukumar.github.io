Junior Thesis
At my high school, every student in the Medical Technology and Health Science Academy is required to write a thesis paper or research paper on the topic of their choice. This research project took me two years, and I conducted my research on solar cells and their implementations in developing countries. The specific focus of this project was to create affordable, low cost solar cookers for people in developing countries. Globally, more than 33% of the population is exposed to carbon dioxide emissions due to cooking over fires. This is extremely hazardous, and this research project sought to find a solution through the implementation of solar immersion heaters. Most of this research was conducted at California Polytechnic University, under the guidance of the Physics Department. However, outside of the university setting, I was able to develop a solar cooker that cost lest than a $2 to build and costed $0.70. Commericially, these heaters cost around $15, and even more in developing countries. I also analyzed the sociological impact on countries that would potentially be able to implement this technology.
